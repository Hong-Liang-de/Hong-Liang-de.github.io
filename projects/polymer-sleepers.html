Dynamic Analysis of Polymer Railway Sleepers

A finite element investigation of recycled polymer railway sleepers subjected to moving train loads.

← Back to portfolio

Overview

This project investigates the dynamic behaviour of recycled polymer railway sleepers within a ballastless track system. The numerical model includes the rail, rail pad, sleeper, foundation support, and a moving wheel load.

Research questions

The work examines how sleeper stiffness, contact conditions, loading speed, and preload influence rail deflection, sleeper stresses, and the overall dynamic response of the track.

Modelling approach

The model is developed in ABAQUS using solid elements, spring or connector-based support, moving contact, and implicit/ explicit dynamic analysis. Particular attention is given to stable load transfer and interpretation of contact pressure, connector forces, and sleeper stress fields.

Next steps

Planned extensions include parameter studies, validation against literature, and comparison of polymer sleepers with conventional solutions.

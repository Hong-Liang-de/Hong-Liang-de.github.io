Overview

At BAM, I support research involving X-ray computed tomography and in-situ mechanical testing. The work contributes to the development of experimental setups and imaging workflows for observing internal material behaviour under load.

Focus areas

Relevant topics include flat-field correction, phase-contrast tomography, image quality, tensile-rig development, digital image correlation, and the transition from conventional XCT to in-situ CT.

Connection to computation

A long-term objective is to connect internal imaging data with finite element modelling, material identification, and validation of computational mechanics models.

Research direction

This experience motivates my interest in imaging-informed mechanics, multiscale modelling, and the integration of experiments with numerical simulation.
